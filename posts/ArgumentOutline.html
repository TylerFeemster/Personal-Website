Problem: It's clear that there is something and not nothing. Why? Explanation: The question is not yes vs no, something vs nothing. The wonder that first provokes the question is the rich particularity of our life. Why this particular existence and not just nothing at all?

Problem: If we reduce every phenomenon to its fundamental essence, is there not still some particularity that must be explained? Explanation: In other words, there doesn't seem to be any atomic reasons or natural axioms, a priori. If this is the underlying goal (to root things in natural axioms), we should have some conception of what counts as a natural axiom.
